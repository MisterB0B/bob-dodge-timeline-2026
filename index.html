Integrated App Ecosystem

The Light Park operates on a seamless, custom-built app system where Show Builder, Prop Bible, Shop Manager, and Trucking Tracker all tie together. This integrated ecosystem ensures data flows seamlessly from design → inventory → warehouse → trucks → venues, eliminating duplicate entry and manual coordination.

Show Builder

Master inventory + RFID system for all props across 8 venues

Prop Bible

Technical drawings, assembly instructions, specifications

Shop Manager

Tasks, calendar, inventory tracking, warehouse operations

Trucking Tracker

RFID-generated truck manifests, space-per-prop tracking (alerts when creating packing lists if truck is full), loading zones, fleet tracking

RFID + QR Code Dual System

Why RFID is critical: RFID tags allow truck loaders to scan entire pallets and racks instantly. Without RFID, loaders would have to manually scan 4-5 props on every pallet and unstack large metal coro racks to scan every individual QR code — a process that would take many hours and severely slow down the trucking operation. RFID enables us to know what's in every truck in a timely fashion, making load-out efficient and accurate.

QR codes complement RFID: Matt Thompson is also creating QR codes for every prop. These QR codes tie into our maintenance tracker system. On-site techs scan QR codes to record any maintenance or repairs done on props during the show season. When props return to the warehouse, we immediately know what needs to be worked on — no guesswork, no surprises.

Summary: RFID = fast truck loading/unloading (bulk scanning). QR codes = detailed maintenance tracking at venues. Both created by Matt Thompson, both essential to operations.
